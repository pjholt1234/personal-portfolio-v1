UWE ITS Website

Date: 02/10/2020 - 11/03/21

Description: Within my second year of university, I undertook a module called, “The Information Practitioner 2”. This module involved being placed in a random group and having a task assigned to you, ranging from creating a product using a piece of technology such as block chain, or writing a report. My groups task involved analysing the University of The West of England IT support pages and provide useful information regarding student’s thoughts, general functionality and possible improvements through prototyping and a large final report containing our findings.

We were assigned a “client” which we had to communicate with through meetings and E-Mails to ensure that the “product” we produced was to their liking. I took on the project management role and through this I learnt a great deal about how to communicate professional, organise my team, assign roles and keep to my responsibilities. Building professional relationships with your career is vital and this module served me well in equipping me with the necessary skills.

Alongside these skills, I learnt about interface architecture and website organisation. Additionally, how to take data, captured through primary research, to inform decisions regarding website design choices. This project was a large success as some of the suggested changes were adopted by the UWE ITS team when rebuilding their website that year.
